Implicit Behavioral Alignment of Language Agents in High-Stakes Crowd Simulations

Yunzhe Wang, Gale M. Lucas, Burcin Becerik-Gerber, Volkan Ustun
University of Southern California & USC Institute for Creative Technologies
EMNLP 2025
Paper Code arXiv

PEBA-PEvo framework demonstrates how persona optimization can align language agent behaviors with expert distributions in high-stakes crowd simulations, closing the Behavior-Realism Gap through iterative persona refinement.

Abstract

Language-driven generative agents have enabled large-scale social simulations with transformative uses, from interpersonal training to aiding global policy-making. However, recent studies indicate that generative agent behaviors often deviate from expert expectations and real-world dataâ€”a phenomenon we term the Behavior-Realism Gap. To address this, we introduce a theoretical framework called Persona-Environment Behavioral Alignment (PEBA), formulated as a distribution matching problem grounded in Lewin's behavior equation stating that behavior is a function of the person and their environment. Leveraging PEBA, we propose PersonaEvolve (PEvo), an LLM-based optimization algorithm that iteratively refines agent personas, implicitly aligning their collective behaviors with realistic expert benchmarks within a specified environmental context. We validate PEvo in an active shooter incident simulation we developed, achieving an 84% average reduction in distributional divergence compared to no steering and a 34% improvement over explicit instruction baselines. Results also show PEvo-refined personas generalize to novel, related simulation scenarios. Our method greatly enhances behavioral realism and reliability in high-stakes social simulations. More broadly, the PEBA-PEvo framework provides a principled approach to developing trustworthy LLM-driven social simulations.

PEBA Framework

Figure 1: PEBA-PEvo workflow showing persona optimization cycle

Persona-Environment Behavioral Alignment

PEBA extends Lewin's interactionist thesis by introducing a probabilistic interpretation where behavior is treated as a random variable jointly influenced by persona and environment context.

Key Components:

  • Persona Space (P): Agent characteristics and traits
  • Environment Context (E): Situational factors and constraints
  • Behavior Space (B): Observable actions and decisions
  • Distribution Matching: Aligning simulated with expert distributions

Methodology

PersonaEvolve Algorithm

PEvo operates through an iterative optimization cycle:

  1. Simulation: Generate agent trajectories using current personas
  2. Classification: Map trajectories to high-level behavior classes
  3. Divergence Measurement: Compare simulated vs. expert distributions
  4. Persona Selection: Identify agents contributing most to the gap
  5. Optimization: Refine selected personas using LLM guidance

This process continues until the Behavior-Realism Gap is minimized, ensuring authentic collective behavior emergence.
